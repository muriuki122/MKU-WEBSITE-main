Our Scientific Journey

Our Research Mission

The Center for Placental Research is dedicated to advancing the understanding of placental biology in maternal and fetal health. We decode the complex mechanisms driving placental malaria and host–pathogen interactions.

Global & Local Impact

We investigate how infectious diseases influence pregnancy outcomes in resource-limited settings. Our integrated lab-to-field evidence informs next-gen diagnostics and life-saving interventions.

Training the Future

Committed to strengthening research capacity, we mentor the next generation of African scientists to lead locally grounded, globally relevant scientific discoveries.

Current Research Focus

Cutting-edge projects driving maternal health breakthroughs

Placental Malaria

Mapping infection at cellular resolution — single‑cell transcriptomics reveal how P. falciparum sequesters in placenta, driving preterm birth & low birthweight.

🔬 Single‑cell RNAseq 🧬 Biomarker discovery 💊 Novel therapeutic targets
Explore malaria research →

Congenital Syphilis

Rapid, field-deployable point-of-care tests using CRISPR/Cas technology and smartphone integration for early case detection in remote areas.

📱 Digital health ⚡ 20-min results 🏥 Community screening
Learn about diagnostics →

Single Cell Atlas of Sub‑Saharan Placenta

First high-resolution atlas of healthy and diseased placenta from African cohorts, revealing cellular interactions, immune dysfunction, and novel pregnancy complications pathways.

📊 Spatial transcriptomics 🧬 Open-access resource
